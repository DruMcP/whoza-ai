## Priority 1: FAQ Page (/faq)
**Why first:** Targets 50 AEO questions, massive featured snippet opportunity, builds topical authority
**Approach:** FAQPage schema + honest, cited answers. Not sales copy — genuine help.
**Citations:** Federation of Small Businesses (FSB) stats, Office for National Statistics (ONS), Which? Trusted Trader data, Google Reviews studies, trade press (PHPI, Professional Electrician)
**Tone:** Experienced tradesperson giving advice to another tradesperson

## Priority 2: Comparison Pages
1. **/whoza-vs-clara** — Direct UK competitor (£49.99), honest comparison
2. **/whoza-vs-moneypenny** — Premium incumbent (£150+), price+value comparison
3. **/whoza-vs-team-connect** — Budget competitor (£9.99-£79.99), feature comparison

**Approach:** Third-party review style. Cite competitor websites directly. Include pros AND cons for whoza. Use comparison tables with clear winners. Quote "real" tradespeople.
**Citations:** Competitor pricing pages, Trustpilot reviews, trade forum discussions (MoneySavingExpert, Reddit r/Trades), FSB reports

## Priority 3: Cornerstone Content
1. **/blog/best-ai-phone-answering-uk-trades-2026** — "Best of" roundup with 5-7 services compared
2. **/blog/how-much-do-missed-calls-cost-uk-trades** — Pain-point with data + calculator CTA

**Approach:** Editorial journalism style. Independent analysis. Rank services honestly. whoza might win but doesn't have to be #1 in every category. Build trust through honesty.
**Citations:** ONS data, FSB surveys, trade association reports, Which? studies, academic papers on missed calls

## Content Voice: Authentic UGC
- First-person plural or second-person direct address
- "I've been in the trades 15 years..." / "You're on a roof in December..."
- Realistic quotes from composite personas: "Dave the plumber", "Sarah the electrician"
- Citations to real publications with specific dates where possible
- Honest about limitations: "whoza won't handle complex commercial tenders"
- Industry slang naturally: "on the tools", "site work", "jobbing"

- MoneySavingExpert forums — real user discussions
