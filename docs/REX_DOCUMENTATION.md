# Rex AI Decision Engine

## Overview

Rex is an AI-powered decision engine that uses **Entity Confidence Engineering (ECE)** to increase the likelihood that AI answer engines (like ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini) confidently recommend your business.

Unlike traditional SEO that focuses on ranking pages, Rex focuses on building entity clarity, consensus, and trust signals that AI systems use to make recommendations.

## Core Philosophy

Rex operates on these non-negotiable principles:

1. **ONE recommendation at a time** - Rex never overwhelms with bulk changes
2. **Human approval required** - Rex never takes actions without explicit approval
3. **Conservative & incremental** - Small, low-risk improvements that compound over time
4. **Trust-first approach** - Every recommendation builds legitimacy and verifiability
5. **No automation** - All actions are manual and intentional

## The 5 ECE Pillars

Rex evaluates your business across five critical dimensions:

### 1. Entity Clarity
**What it measures:** Is your business role unambiguous? Is service + location clear and consistent?

**Why it matters:** AI needs to instantly understand what you do and where you do it. Ambiguity kills recommendations.

**Example improvements:**
- Clarify business name to include service type
- Specify exact service areas
- Remove generic terms like "solutions" or "services"

### 2. Consensus Shaping
**What it measures:** Do all platforms describe your business the same way?

**Why it matters:** AI cross-references information. Conflicting descriptions create doubt.

**Example improvements:**
- Align website and profile descriptions
- Ensure consistent service categories across platforms
- Create unified value proposition

### 3. Answer Readiness
**What it measures:** Does your content already sound like direct answers to customer questions?

**Why it matters:** AI favors content that's already in answer format. If your content sounds like how AI would answer a query, it's more likely to be used.

**Example improvements:**
- Rewrite descriptions in Q&A format
- List services explicitly
- Add FAQs in conversational language

### 4. Risk Reduction
**What it measures:** Are credentials explicit and easy to verify?

**Why it matters:** AI assesses risk before recommending. Verifiable trust signals (licenses, certifications, contact info) reduce perceived risk.

**Example improvements:**
- Add phone number and email
- List licenses and certifications
- State clear service boundaries (what you do and don't offer)

### 5. Confidence Accretion
**What it measures:** Are you making regular small improvements over time?

**Why it matters:** Consistent activity signals a reliable, active business. Momentum builds confidence.

**Example improvements:**
- Post regular business updates
- Respond to reviews
- Complete missing profile sections

## How Rex Works

### Step 1: Initial Evaluation
Rex analyzes your business across all 5 ECE pillars and assigns scores (0-100) based on:
- Profile completeness
- Content clarity
- Cross-platform consistency
- Trust signals
- Activity patterns

### Step 2: Identify Weakest Pillar
Rex identifies which pillar needs the most attention. This becomes the focus area.

### Step 3: Generate ONE Recommendation
Rex creates a single, specific, low-risk action targeting the weakest pillar. Each recommendation includes:

- **Title** - Clear, action-oriented
- **Explanation** - Why this matters in plain English
- **Exact Copy** - Copy-paste text when applicable
- **Where to Apply** - Specific location (e.g., Google Business Profile)
- **Expected Impact** - What confidence increase to expect
- **Estimated Time** - Usually 2-5 minutes

### Step 4: Human Approval
The recommendation is reviewed by:
1. Rex admin (internal review)
2. Customer (you approve or decline)

### Step 5: Manual Implementation
You implement the change manually. Rex never publishes on your behalf.

### Step 6: Mark Complete & Re-evaluate
After completion, Rex:
- Records what changed
- Re-evaluates your business
- Measures improvement
- Generates the next recommendation

## Rex Workflow States

### Recommendation Status Flow

```
Draft → Sent to Admin → Sent to Customer → Approved → In Progress → Completed
                                              ↓
                                          Declined
```

**Draft**: Rex has generated a recommendation internally
**Sent to Admin**: Under internal review
**Sent to Customer**: Awaiting your approval
**Approved**: Ready for you to implement
**In Progress**: You're working on it
**Completed**: Done and recorded in history
**Declined**: You chose not to implement (with reason)

## Using Rex in Your Portal

### Access Rex
1. Log into your portal
2. Click the **Rex AI** tab
3. View your Entity Confidence Evaluation

### Run Initial Evaluation
Click **Run Initial Evaluation** to have Rex analyze your business for the first time.

### Review Your Scores
View your 5 pillar scores. The lowest score is your "weakest pillar" and will be addressed first.

### Generate Recommendation
Click **Generate Recommendation** to have Rex create ONE specific action.

### Approve or Decline
Review the recommendation carefully:
- Read the explanation
- Copy the exact text (if provided)
- Understand where to apply it
- Note the expected impact

Click **Approve & Start** or **Decline** (with reason).

### Implement the Change
Follow the instructions to make the change manually:
1. Go to the specified location (e.g., Google Business Profile)
2. Paste the exact copy or make the described change
3. Verify it's live

### Mark as Complete
Return to Rex and click **Mark as Completed**:
- Describe what you changed
- Note any immediate impact
- Submit

Rex will re-evaluate your business and generate the next recommendation.

**Where to Apply:** Add this to your Google Business Profile services section

**Where to Apply:** Add this to your Google Business Profile business description
**Expected Impact:** Provides verifiable proof of legitimacy. AI prioritizes businesses with explicit credentials.

## Technical Details

### Database Schema
Rex uses 5 main tables:
- `rex_evaluation_criteria` - Configurable ECE assessment criteria
- `rex_ece_evaluations` - Historical evaluations with pillar scores
- `rex_recommendations` - Active and historical recommendations
- `rex_action_history` - Completed actions with impact measurements
- `rex_confidence_scores` - Time-series confidence tracking

### Key Constraint
**Only ONE active recommendation per business.** This is enforced at the database level with a partial unique index.

### Evaluation Algorithm
Rex scores each pillar (0-100) based on:
- Profile data completeness
- Content quality and format
- Cross-platform consistency
- Trust signal presence
- Activity recency

The weakest scoring pillar becomes the target for the next recommendation.

### Action Generation
Rex uses rule-based logic to generate recommendations:
1. Identify weakest pillar
2. Analyze specific gaps in that pillar
3. Select the smallest, most impactful action
4. Generate context-specific instructions
5. Create copy-paste text when possible

## Support & Questions

### Why only ONE recommendation?
Overwhelm leads to inaction. ONE clear action is more likely to be completed than a list of 10.

### Can I request a specific type of recommendation?
Currently, Rex autonomously selects based on weakest pillar. Future versions may allow preferences.

### What if I disagree with a recommendation?
Decline it with a reason. Your feedback helps Rex learn what works for your business.

### How long until I see results?
Entity confidence compounds over time. Expect to see AI mentions increasing after 10-15 completed actions (typically 2-3 months).

### Can Rex publish changes for me?
No. Rex never logs into accounts or publishes on your behalf. This is a core principle.
