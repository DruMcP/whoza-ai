# 1. Executive Summary

This briefing consolidates the core Whoza.ai commercial model, the Trillet white-label infrastructure advantages, the client onboarding and knowledge-file architecture, and the required agentic infrastructure so the development agents can incorporate these requirements into the master product plan going forward.

The core strategic point is simple: Whoza must not be built as an AI receptionist. It must be built as an AI revenue operations system for UK trades. It captures demand, qualifies enquiries, delivers them by WhatsApp, collects reviews through Claire, analyses competitors and AI visibility through Rex, and produces weekly actions that help clients win more work.

- Whoza public positioning: The AI revenue team for UK trades.
- Core product loop: Capture calls -> qualify enquiries -> deliver by WhatsApp -> collect reviews -> analyse competitors -> recommend actions -> generate more work.
- Trillet should remain invisible to end customers; Whoza packages the capability as its own platform.
- The Client Knowledge Profile is the source of truth for Katie, Mark, Claire and Rex, especially for clients without websites.
- The infrastructure must support website scrape onboarding, no-website onboarding, ongoing learning from accepted/rejected enquiries, and tiered feature access.

# 2. Strategic Foundation

## 2.1 Whoza is not an AI receptionist

Whoza should be described and engineered as a complete revenue system. Basic AI receptionist platforms answer calls. Whoza should capture demand from calls and social leads, send qualified enquiries to the tradesperson, automate review growth, and provide weekly growth actions from competitor and AI visibility analysis.

## 2.2 Core customer promise

Whoza helps UK trades stop missing calls, respond faster, capture real customer enquiries, improve reputation, and understand what to fix next to get more calls and more booked work.

## 2.3 Core platform loop

Call or lead received -> Katie/Mark answers and qualifies -> enquiry sent to WhatsApp -> tradesperson accepts/declines -> job completed -> Claire requests review -> Rex analyses competitors and AI visibility -> weekly actions delivered -> more visibility, more calls, more booked jobs.

# 6. Client Knowledge Profile - Core Infrastructure Requirement

Every client requires a Client Knowledge Profile. This is the operational source of truth that powers Katie, Mark, Claire and Rex. It must work for clients with strong websites, poor websites, listings only, or no digital presence.

# 7. Onboarding Paths

## 7.1 Path A - Client has a proper website

1. Client submits website URL and business contact information.
1. System scrapes website and public reviews/listings.
1. AI drafts Client Knowledge Profile.
1. Client confirms services, area, hours, pricing rules and tone.
1. Katie/Mark test call is generated.
1. Client approves and goes live.

## 7.2 Path B - Client has weak/no website but has listings

1. Client submits business name, postcode, Google Business Profile link, Facebook page or listing links.
1. System pulls public listing data where available.
1. AI drafts knowledge profile from listings, reviews and onboarding answers.
1. Client confirms missing fields in a checklist.
1. Test call and WhatsApp enquiry preview are approved.

## 7.3 Path C - Client has no usable online presence

This is not a blocker. It becomes a Whoza advantage. Use the message: No website? No problem. We build your AI call profile during onboarding.

1. Client completes no-website onboarding wizard.
1. System creates structured knowledge profile from form answers.
1. Support agent reviews for missing risk fields.
1. Katie/Mark test call is produced and approved.
1. Client goes live with standard qualification rules.

# 8. Agent-Specific Knowledge Separation

# 9. Ongoing Learning Loop

The key moat is that Whoza should learn from accepted and rejected enquiries. Every WhatsApp Accept/Decline decision should improve the client profile.

1. After every enquiry, ask: Was this a good enquiry? Good / Not suitable.
1. If rejected, ask reason: out of area, too small, wrong service, price shopper, unavailable, other.
1. Update preferred jobs, rejected jobs, service area, minimum job value and qualification rules.
1. Feed learnings into Katie/Mark scripts, Rex recommendations and dashboard reports.
1. Log every profile change with timestamp, source and approval status.

# 10. Data Architecture Requirements

The database should support client profiles, agent-specific knowledge, onboarding status, trial usage, call events, enquiries, reviews, Rex reports and audit logs. Supabase is the recommended system of record.

# 11. Onboarding UX Requirements

- The first onboarding screen must ask if the client has a website, Google Business Profile or social/listing profile.
- No-website path must be equally valid and framed positively.
- Clients must approve the knowledge profile before go-live.
- Clients must hear a Katie or Mark test call before go-live.
- Clients must confirm WhatsApp destination, escalation contact and review link.
- Trial should only activate once minimum knowledge and contact-routing fields are complete.

# 12. Guardrails for Agent Responses

- Do not quote fixed prices unless approved in the client profile.
- Do not guarantee arrival times unless approved.
- Do not diagnose technical problems beyond triage questions.
- Do not accept work outside service area or excluded services.
- Use safe wording: I will pass this to the team for confirmation.
- Escalate urgent or high-risk issues using the client escalation rules.
