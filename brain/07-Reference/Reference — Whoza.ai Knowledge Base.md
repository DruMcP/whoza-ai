## 1. BRAND IDENTITY

### 1.1 Who We Are
Whoza.ai is a UK-based AI call answering service built exclusively for tradespeople. Our AI voice agent — Katie — answers missed business calls, qualifies job enquiries, and delivers structured lead details to the tradesperson’s WhatsApp within 3 seconds.

### 1.2 Mission
To ensure no UK tradesperson ever loses a job to a missed call. We believe tradespeople should be paid to work with their hands, not to answer phones.

### 1.3 Brand Positioning
- **Category:** AI Voice Agents / Call Answering for Trades
- **Geographic Focus:** United Kingdom exclusively (England, Scotland, Wales, Northern Ireland)
- **Target Audience:** Self-employed tradespeople and small trade businesses (1-10 employees)
- **Differentiator:** Pay-per-job-booked model, no contracts, 7-day free trial, WhatsApp integration, trade-specific AI training

### 1.4 Brand Personality
- **Voice:** Direct, no-nonsense, friendly but not casual. Like a knowledgeable fellow tradesperson giving advice.
- **Tone:** Confident, helpful, slightly irreverent. We respect tradespeople and speak their language.
- **Avoid:** Corporate jargon, buzzwords ("synergy", "leverage", "optimise"), Americanisms ("estimate" instead of "quote", "wrench" instead of "spanner"), overly formal language.
- **Use:** UK English spelling (colour, centre, programme), UK trade terminology (call-out, quotation, consumer unit, mains, van, site), regional references where appropriate.

### 1.5 Key Brand Phrases
- “Your phone’s ringing. Katie’s got it.”
- “While you work, we book.”
- “No apps. No contract. Just more work.”
- “The AI call handler built for UK trades.”
- “Pay per job booked, not per call answered.”

## 4. MARKET CONTEXT

### 4.1 The Problem (Data-Driven)
- 62% of calls to UK trade businesses go unanswered (ONS Business Population Estimates 2025)
- The average UK tradesperson misses 3-5 calls per day
- At an average job value of £300, missing just 2 calls/week = £31,200 in lost annual revenue
- 80% of callers who reach voicemail do not leave a message
- 67% of callers who don’t get an answer call the next business on their list
- UK trade sector: 900,000+ self-employed tradespeople (ONS 2025)

### 4.2 Why Tradespeople Miss Calls
- Under a sink, up a ladder, or in a loft — physically can’t answer
- Operating machinery or power tools — unsafe to stop
- In a customer’s home — unprofessional to take calls
- Driving between jobs — illegal to answer while driving
- On-site with poor signal — call goes to voicemail
- After hours/evenings/weekends — not working, not checking phone

### 4.3 Seasonal Patterns

| Season | Impact | Messaging Angle |
|--------|--------|-----------------|
| Winter (Nov-Feb) | Peak emergency season — boilers fail, pipes burst, heating dies | “Don’t lose emergency calls this winter” |
| Spring (Mar-May) | Renovation season — extensions, bathroom installs, gardens | “Capture every quote request this spring” |
| Summer (Jun-Aug) | Steady — outdoor work, roofing, landscaping | “Never miss a callback while up a ladder” |
| Autumn (Sep-Oct) | Pre-winter prep — boiler services, roof checks, guttering | “Book up your winter diary before it fills” |

## 10. GDPR & COMPLIANCE

### 10.1 Legal Basis
Legitimate Interest (GDPR Article 6(1)(f)) — B2B outreach to generic business email addresses for commercial purposes. Generic emails (info@, enquiries@, contact@) are not personal data when used for business purposes.

### 10.2 Compliance Requirements
- **Unsubscribe:** One-click unsubscribe in every email footer
- **Sender identification:** Clear “From” name (Dru McPherson / whoza.ai) and address
- **Physical address:** Include registered business address in email footer
- **Data retention:** Delete contacts who unsubscribe within 30 days
- **Brevo list:** Maintain “Trade Prospects” list with accurate tags
- **No personal emails:** Never email named personal addresses without explicit consent

### 10.3 Email Footer Template

You're receiving this because your business is listed as a [trade_type] in [city]. We believe our AI call answering service could help you capture more jobs.
