# Research — Brand Strength Analysis

## Summary
whoza.ai is a **C+/B- brand** for the trades market. Tech-forward and modern, but underperforms on trades-focus and trust. The name is abstract — it neither communicates "AI voice" nor "trades" nor "UK." Salvageable and worth keeping because the .ai TLD signals modernity and the name doesn't pigeonhole us into voice-only.

## Brand Scorecard

| Dimension | Score | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Memorability | C+ | "whoza" is short but not sticky. "Trade Receptionist" and "CallChimps" tell a story. |
| Descriptiveness | D+ | Does not communicate "AI voice," "trades," "UK," or "receptionist." |
| Trust/Credibility | C | .ai TLD signals "tech company" — tradespeople may distrust. |
| Differentiation | B | Stands out from literal names (Trade Receptionist, VoiceFleet). |
| Scalability | B+ | Not locked into "voice" or "receptionist." Can hold the bundle. |
| Searchability | C | "whoza" has no search volume. All brand awareness must be paid or earned. |
| Domain strength | B | whoza.ai is short, typeable, already secured. |
| Phonetic clarity | C+ | "hoo-za" or "who-za"? Tradespeople will hesitate. |
| Age resonance | C | 35–55 tradespeople prefer straightforward, no-nonsense names. |
| Local/UK flavour | D | No British identity whatsoever. |

**Overall: C+/B- (2.7/4.0 GPA)**

## Trades Market Psychology

### Who We're Selling To
- **Age:** 35–55 (Gen X / elder Millennial)
- **Personality:** Practical, time-poor, skeptical of "tech solutions"
- **Decision style:** "Does it make me money or save me time?"
- **Social proof:** Trust what other tradespeople say, not what brands claim
- **Brand preference:** Straightforward, honest, local, proven

### What They Respond To
- "Never miss a call again" — clear outcome
- "Built for plumbers" — identity match
- "UK-based" — local trust
- "Other plumbers use this" — social proof
- No jargon, no "AI," no "platform" — unless explained with clear money/save context

### What whoza.ai Signals
- "What's a whoza?" — confusion
- ".ai — is this some tech thing?" — skepticism
- "Sounds like one of them startup things" — distrust
- "Probably goes bust in a year" — common objection

## Recommended Fixes
1. **Killer tagline:** "Never Miss a Customer Call Again" (does the work the name doesn't)
2. **Trade-specific landing pages:** "AI Phone Answering for Plumbers in Manchester"
3. **Social proof density:** Google Reviews badge (5.0/15), testimonials front and centre
4. **Local UK signals:** UK phone numbers, British voice personas, local case studies
5. **"Built for trades" messaging:** Not "AI platform" but "your 24/7 receptionist"
