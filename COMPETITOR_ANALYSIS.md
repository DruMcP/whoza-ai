# Competitor Analysis: Whoza.ai

## Executive Summary

**Critical Finding: No direct competitor offers the exact whoza.ai model.**

Whoza.ai operates in a unique position between traditional lead-generation platforms and enterprise AI SEO tools. There is no other platform offering AI visibility scoring + weekly task assistant + competitor analysis specifically for tradespeople at a £59/month flat fee.

The closest competitors either:
- Charge per-lead (expensive, unpredictable) OR
- Charge $100-300+/month (enterprise tools, too technical) OR
- Only monitor AI visibility (no actionable tasks or assistant)

## Competitive Moats

### 1. Trade-Specific Focus
No competitor is built specifically for tradespeople. SE Ranking, Semrush, Ahrefs are generic SEO tools. Checkatrade is a directory. This focus creates:
- Better task relevance ("Add this to your Google Business Profile" not "Optimize your hreflang tags")
- Better messaging ("See who AI recommends for YOUR trade")
- Better community (tradespeople trust tradespeople)

### 2. The "Rex" Assistant Model
No competitor offers a weekly task assistant. They give you dashboards; Rex gives you a to-do list. For tradespeople who:
- Don't understand SEO data
- Don't have time to interpret reports
- Want someone to tell them exactly what to do

Rex is the unique differentiator.

### 3. Price Point
At £59/month, Whoza.ai is:
- 30-60% cheaper than Checkatrade
- 30-50% cheaper than AI SEO monitoring tools
- 80-90% cheaper than SEO agencies
- Only 3x more expensive than pure monitoring tools (but includes tasks)

This is the sweet spot — affordable enough for a sole trader, valuable enough to retain.

### 4. No Per-Lead Fees
Unlike MyBuilder, Bark, and Rated People, Whoza.ai has no per-lead costs. Tradespeople hate unpredictable expenses. A flat £59/month is budgetable.

## Strategic Recommendations

### 1. Own the Category Name
No competitor calls it "AI Visibility Score." Trademark this concept. When tradespeople think "How visible am I in AI?" they should think "I need my Whoza score."

### 2. Emphasise the Rex Assistant
The assistant model is unique. Competitors have dashboards; you have a partner. Lean into this heavily in marketing.

### 3. Price Anchoring
Always show the comparison: *"SEO agencies charge £500-2000/month. Checkatrade charges £90-140/month. Whoza.ai builds your own visibility channel for £59/month."*

### 4. Lock In Trade-Specific IP
Build trade-specific task libraries (plumber tasks, electrician tasks, builder tasks). This makes it harder for generic SEO tools to compete.

### 5. Move Fast
The AI visibility space is 12-18 months old. First-mover advantage in the trades vertical is critical. Build brand recognition before SE Ranking or Semrush notices this niche.
